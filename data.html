Project Background and Data

The concept for my independent work project is based on W.E.B Duboisâ€™ 1890s series of infographics depicting different representations of inequality at the time. Some of his images incorporated spirals to show the vast disparities. I was inspired by his use of the spiral because it represented a new and innovative method of visualization at the time.

I collected the data for this project first by researching and compiling information about the fifty most followed TikTok accounts, such as their follower counts, average number of comments per video, as well as race and gender demographic information. I also did some web scraping of video comment sections to compile files of comments which I then evaluated to find a positivity rating for. This rating was found by training a sentiment analysis program on comment section data and then inputing the comments for the video of each evaluated TikToker.
